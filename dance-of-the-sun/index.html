The centre of the Solar System is known as the barycentre. It isn't quite the same as the centre of the Sun. Sometimes the centre of the Solar System isn't even within the Sun. The barycentre is the gravitational balance point a bit like the pivot point on a seesaw. The vast majority of mass in the Solar System is in the Sun but there is some in the planets and this acts to pull the balance point away from the Sun's centre. The motion of the Sun around the centre of the Solar System is a complicated path determined by the motions of the planets.
